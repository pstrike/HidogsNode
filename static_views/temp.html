  • If a pet has or had any condition, whether diagnosed by your veterinarian or not, before, during or after a Vacay, it is considered pre-existing. This can include: respiratory infections, urinary tract/bladder infections, blood disorders, vomiting, feline immunodeficiency or leukemia virus, diarrhea, glaucoma and other gastrointestinal disorders.
  • If a pet has been diagnosed with a disease of an anatomical area where the underlying cause was undetermined any diagnosis of the same area would not be covered. For example, gastritis and colitis are both general diagnoses of inflammation, but do not get at the cause of the disorder.

If your pet has had any of the following conditions prior to staying with a Host as part of such Host’s Host-Provided Services (a “Vacay”) or your pet is diagnosed with any of these conditions our Insurance Program will not apply:

Any orthopedic condition including but not limited to luxating patellas, hip dysplasia, elbow dysplasia, and OCD, orthopedic illness or injury on the opposite side of a prior injury. Chronic conditions including, but not limited to, allergies, cancer, diabetes, lipomas or skin lumps, hyperthyroidism or hypothyroidism, urinary or bladder crystals or blockage.

  • Breed-specific conditions: A genetic condition is caused by a defect in the underlying genes that are transmitted to a puppy or kitten from its parents. These gene defects run in families and some breeds have many more of them than other breeds, hence the term breed-specific condition.

Note: Treatment for many breed-specific conditions requires corrective surgery and some are also chronic conditions that require ongoing care. Among the most common breed-specific conditions are: hip and elbow dysplasia (common in German Shepherds, Golden Retrievers, Labrador Retrievers, Great Danes and other large/giant breed dogs), patellar luxation (common in Poodles, Yorkshire Terriers, Pomeranians, Chihuahuas, Boston Terriers, Cavalier King Charles and other toy/small breed dogs), interverterbral disc disease (IVDD) Spinal (ruptured, herniated) disc issues (common in Dachshunds, Bassett Hounds, French Bulldogs, Pomeranians, Corgis, Pugs and other chondrodystrophic breeds), cancer, allergies.

  • Chronic conditions: Chronic conditions persist or last for several months (often for years). They generally cannot be prevented by vaccines or cured by medication, nor do they just disappear. Common conditions include: allergies, cancer, diabetes, IBD (inflammatory bowel disease), Addison's disease, Cushing's disease, dry eyes (KCS), epilepsy, glaucoma, hypothyroidism, Orthopedic issues such as arthritis, IVDD & cruciate ligament tears.
